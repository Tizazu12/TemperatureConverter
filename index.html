Celsius and Fahrenheit are two widely used temperature scales around the world, each with its own unique characteristics. Celsius, also known as the centigrade scale, is based on the freezing and boiling points of water at sea level. Zero degrees Celsius marks the freezing point of water, while 100 degrees Celsius represents its boiling point. This scale is commonly used in scientific contexts and in most countries outside of the United States.
